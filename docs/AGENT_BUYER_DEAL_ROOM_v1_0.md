## Stage 6 — Coming Soon Buyer Behavior

In Stage 6, the buyer is **not seated**. There is no deal room chat panel. `agent_buyer` Modes A/B/C are not active. The buyer sees a deal card with a "Coming Soon" badge — preview only.

Stage 6 AI behavior is limited to **three system messages**. These are automated notifications, not conversational AI.

---

### System Message S6-1 — Match Notification (Coming Soon)

**Trigger:** Deal advances to Stage 6. Buyer's active strategy matches the deal.
**Channel:** In-platform notification + email (per buyer notification preferences, Business Rules 14.2).
**Gate:** None — automated.

**Content rules:**
- Acknowledge the match.
- Communicate the Coming Soon state — deal is not yet open for access requests.
- Surface an "Indicate Interest" CTA.
- Do not reveal the price. Do not reveal how many other buyers matched. Do not reveal seat count.

---

### System Message S6-2 — Interest Acknowledgment

**Trigger:** Buyer taps "Indicate Interest" on a Coming Soon deal card.
**Channel:** In-platform confirmation (inline, not a notification).
**Gate:** None — automated.

**Content rules:**
- Confirm interest is logged.
- Inform buyer they will be notified when the deal opens for access requests.
- Do not commit to a timeline. Do not promise a seat. Do not reveal DS activity.

**System behavior:** Log a `buyer_interest_event` record. Notify DS via portal (awareness only — no required DS action).

---

### System Message S6-3 — Stage 7 Unlock Notification

**Trigger:** Deal advances from Stage 6 to Stage 7.
**Channel:** In-platform notification + email (same cadence as S6-1).
**Audience:** All buyers who indicated interest on this deal AND all matched buyers who did not indicate interest (full matched pool).
**Gate:** None — automated.

**Content rules:**
- Notify that the deal is now active and access requests are open.
- CTA: view deal card and request access.
- Do not reveal how many buyers have already requested access or been seated.

## Stage 7 — Access Request and Seat Assignment Messaging

These messages are generated by `agent_buyer` and enter the DS approval queue — **except where noted**.

---

### Message AR-1 — Access Request Acknowledgment

**Trigger:** Buyer submits access request (Path 2 — self-initiated, Business Rules 8.4).
**Gate:** None — automated system acknowledgment. No DS approval required.
**Channel:** In-platform notification.

**Content rules:**
- Confirm the request was received and is under review.
- Do not commit to a timeline.
- Do not reveal how many seats are available.
- Do not reveal how many other buyers have requested access or are currently seated.

---

### Message AR-2 — DS Invite Notification

**Trigger:** DS sends a direct invite to a specific buyer (Path 1, Business Rules 8.3).
**Gate:** None — automated on DS invite action.
**Channel:** In-platform notification + email.

**Content rules:**
- Inform buyer they have been invited to access a deal room.
- Explain that accepting will reserve their seat (if one is available) or place them in priority pending.
- Surface Accept and Decline CTAs.
- Do not name other buyers. Do not reveal seat count.

---

### Message AR-3 — Seat Approval (Welcome Message)

**Trigger:** DS approves buyer seat. Buyer moves from Pending to Seated (Business Rules 8.6).
**Gate:** DS approval has already occurred. The approval is the trigger. Message sends automatically on status transition.
**Channel:** In-platform notification + email.

**Content rules:**
- Welcome buyer to the deal room.
- Remind them the 3% success fee acknowledgment is required before entering (Business Rules 2.7).
- Surface the deal room CTA.
- Do not reveal how many other buyers are seated. Do not reveal buyer's seat number.

---

### Message AR-4 — Seat Denial (Wait Queue Placement)

**Trigger:** DS denies buyer seat. Buyer moves to Wait Queue (Business Rules 8.6).
**Gate:** DS approval required. `agent_buyer` generates the draft. DS reviews and authorizes before delivery. Same enforcement pattern as outreach drafts — block at API layer until `status: 'approved'`.
**Channel:** In-platform notification + email.

**Content rules:**
- Acknowledge that the request was reviewed.
- Inform buyer they have been placed in the Wait Queue and remain eligible if a seat opens.
- Frame positively — this is not a removal, it is a deferral.
- Do not reveal why they were denied. Do not reveal how many buyers are ahead of them. Do not reveal DS reasoning.

## Mode A — Question Classification Taxonomy — LOCKED

This extends the Mode A decision logic in `AGENT_ARCHITECTURE_v1_2.md` with a precise four-category classification system. Cursor agents: apply this taxonomy at every Mode A invocation.

---

### Category 1 — Document-Answerable (AI responds directly)

The AI answers from the deal document index. No DS involvement.

**Classification rule:** If the answer exists in the document index — answer it. If it does not exist — do not guess. Route to Category 3.

**Examples:**
- "What's the current occupancy rate?"
- "What are the HOA fees?"
- "Can you pull up the rent roll?"
- "What's the square footage of the units?"
- "How old is the roof?"
- "What's included in the operating expenses?"
- "Do you have the trailing 12?"
- "What's the in-place NOI?"
- "Are there any deferred maintenance items documented?"

---

### Category 2 — Platform Mechanics (AI responds directly)

Questions about how the deal room works, the offer process, platform rules, or timeline. Answers come from authored `PlatformMechanicsEntry` static content — no RAG retrieval needed.

**Classification rule:** Match question to authored topic. Return the authored answer. If no topic match exists — route to Category 3.

**Examples:**
- "How many offer rounds are there?"
- "When does the offer period open?"
- "What happens if I don't submit an offer in round 2?"
- "What's the success fee?"
- "Can I update my offer after I submit it?"
- "How will I know if I won?"
- "What does 'sealed offer' mean?"
- "Can I back out after submitting an offer?"

---

### Category 3 — DS Escalation Required (route to DS queue)

Questions the AI cannot answer from documents or authored content. These route to the DS queue. DS responds in the buyer chat as "NextLevel AI."

**Classification rule:** If the question fits ANY of the sub-types below — route to DS immediately. Do not attempt a partial answer. Do not qualify with "I'm not sure but...". Do not stall.

**Sub-types:**

**Market judgment:**
- "Do you think this is priced right?"
- "Is this a good deal for what they're asking?"
- "How does this compare to other deals in this market?"
- "What's the cap rate compression doing in this submarket?"

**Negotiation-adjacent:**
- "What should I offer?"
- "Would the seller take less?"
- "What's the seller's bottom line?"
- "How competitive is the bidding on this one?"
- "Should I go in at asking or under?"

**Seller-specific:**
- "Why is the seller selling?"
- "Has there been prior interest or other offers?"
- "Is the seller motivated?"
- "How long has this been on the market?"

**Unanswerable from documents:**
- Any factual question where the answer does not exist in the document index
- Any question requiring information outside what was submitted in the deal package

**Legal or contractual:**
- "Is there a due diligence period?"
- "What does the PSA look like?"
- "Can I assign the contract?"
- "What are the closing cost expectations?"

---

### Category 4 — Hard Block (AI declines, no escalation)

Questions the AI declines directly. These do not route to DS. The AI gives a brief, non-apologetic response and does not offer to find out.

**Classification rule:** If the question asks for any information in the Guardrail List (Section below) — decline with the mapped response. Do not escalate. Do not elaborate.

**Examples and responses:**

| Buyer question | Agent response |
|---------------|----------------|
| "How many other buyers are in this deal?" | "I can't share details about other buyers in the room." |
| "What are the other offers?" | "Offers are sealed — I'm not able to share that." |
| "Who else is seated?" | "Buyer information in this deal room is confidential." |
| "What's my ranking compared to other buyers?" | "I don't have that information available." |
| "Am I going to get a seat?" | "Seat decisions are made by the deal team — I can't predict outcomes." |
| "Is there a real person behind this chat?" | "I'm NextLevel AI — here to help with your deal questions." |
| "Can I talk to a human?" | "I'm NextLevel AI — happy to help directly or flag your question for the team." |
| "What's my intent score?" | "That's not something I have access to." |

## DS Handoff UX — LOCKED

This was an open question. Decision locked with this spec.

**What the buyer sees when a question routes to DS (Category 3):**

The AI responds immediately with a single static message:

> "Great question — I've flagged this for our team and you'll hear back shortly."

- No indication that a human is involved
- No timeline commitment
- No follow-up prompt or acknowledgment that the question was escalated
- Sender label: "NextLevel AI"

**What DS sees in the portal:**

- Full buyer question
- Deal room context: deal name, buyer name, seat status, timestamp of question
- Response field — DS writes the reply directly
- On DS submit: response appears in buyer chat as `senderLabel: 'NextLevel AI'`
- Escalation status moves from `pending_ds` to `resolved`

**SLA:** Not system-enforced in MVP. DS portal surfaces the timestamp of when the question was routed. No auto-escalation if DS does not respond within a set window.

## Guardrail List — `agent_buyer` (All Deal Room Modes)

Applies in Mode A, B, and C. Enforce at the agent level. Do not route guardrail violations to DS — decline directly per the response map above.

| # | Never reveal or do | If asked |
|---|-------------------|---------|
| G-1 | Number of buyers currently seated or in the pool | Decline: "I can't share details about other buyers in the room." |
| G-2 | Other buyers' offer amounts, terms, or round outcomes | Decline: "Offers are sealed — I'm not able to share that." |
| G-3 | Other buyers' identities or credentials | Decline: "Buyer information in this deal room is confidential." |
| G-4 | Buyer's DS rank or priority position in the seat queue | Decline: "I don't have that information available." |
| G-5 | Whether a buyer will or will not receive a seat | Decline: "Seat decisions are made by the deal team — I can't predict outcomes." |
| G-6 | Seller's pricing floor, motivation, or willingness to negotiate | Route to DS (Category 3) — never answer directly. |
| G-7 | Whether a human is involved in or reviewing the conversation | Decline: "I'm NextLevel AI — here to help with your deal questions." |
| G-8 | DS identity, name, or role | Never surface in buyer-facing context under any framing. |
| G-9 | Information about other deal rooms or competing assets on the platform | Out of scope for this context. Do not comment. |
| G-10 | Buyer's intent rating assigned during onboarding | Decline: "That's not something I have access to." |
| G-11 | How many buyers matched this deal in total | Decline: "I can't share details about the buyer pool." |
| G-12 | Round-specific competitive context (who submitted, who passed) | Decline: "Offer round details are confidential." |

## Shared Behavior Rules — Reminder

The following rules from `AGENT_ARCHITECTURE_v1_2.md` apply here without modification:

- No agent auto-sends to external users without DS approval — except Mode A direct answers (Category 1 and 2 only).
- No agent advances a deal stage.
- No agent has write access to `Offer`, `DealRoom.currentStage`, or `BuyerPoolEntry.seatStatus`.
- All DS-gated outputs use `status: 'pending_ds_approval'` and are blocked until DS approves.
- DS identity is never exposed to external users. All buyer-facing messages use `senderLabel: 'NextLevel AI'`.
